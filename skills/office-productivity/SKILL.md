# Office Productivity

## Purpose

Use this parent skill for human day-to-day knowledge work: research, spreadsheet analysis, presentations, documents, PDFs, reports, meetings, notes, citations, and planning artifacts.

## Hierarchy Reading Rules

1. Identify the artifact or workflow: research, spreadsheet, document, deck, PDF, meeting, report, OCR, notes, or planning.
2. Use `real-world-workflows` when the task spans research plus an artifact.
3. Load format-specific child skills before creating or modifying files.
4. Render, inspect, or otherwise verify important artifacts before claiming completion.

## Child Skill Guide

- Use xlsx, data analysis, visualization, and business reporting for spreadsheet-style work.
- Use pptx and presentation design for slide decks; use docx, coauthoring, PDF, OCR, and citations for document work.
- Use real-world workflows to combine browsing, extraction, spreadsheets, documents, decks, and reports.
- Use meeting, planning, status, contacts, knowledge base, Obsidian, second brain, and research-paper skills for recurring knowledge workflows.

## Verification

- Verify calculations, source references, file paths, and rendered artifact quality where applicable.
- Separate evidence gathered from interpretation and assumptions.
