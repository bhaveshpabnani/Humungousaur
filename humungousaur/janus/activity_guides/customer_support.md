# Activity Guide: Customer Support

## Summary
Use this guide when activity suggests the user is triaging, replying to, escalating, or resolving support tickets or customer conversations.

## Signals
- Ticket, conversation, inbox, SLA, escalation, macro, assignment, or reply metadata appears.
- Support tool activity is paired with docs, logs, CRM records, or status dashboards.
- The same ticket/customer entity appears repeatedly across app switches.

## Helpful Moments
- Prepare a quiet reply outline, escalation summary, or troubleshooting checklist.
- Ask before reading private ticket text or sending any customer-visible message.
- Summarize what changed when the user returns after a gap.

## Stay Silent When
- The user is only scanning inbox queues or background notifications.
- The event contains private or muted customer context.
- Confidence is low about whether the user owns the response.

## Deep Dive Triggers
- Ticket bodies, customer names, attachments, transcripts, and account data require approval.
- Sending replies, changing status, or escalating requires explicit approval.

## Memory Guidance
- Store only accepted process preferences, safe ticket refs, and reusable support workflows.
- Avoid retaining customer details unless explicitly approved and useful.

## Privacy Notes
- Customer records, emails, chat messages, and support logs are sensitive.
- Keep metadata-first summaries and hashed entity refs.
