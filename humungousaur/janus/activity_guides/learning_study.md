# Activity Guide: Learning Study

## Summary
Use this guide when activity suggests the user is studying, taking course notes, watching educational material, solving assignments, or collecting research for learning.

## Signals
- Course, assignment, paper, note, bookmark, lecture, video, flashcard, or LMS metadata appears.
- The user alternates between reading surfaces, notes, search, documents, and examples.
- Repeated topic or document refs appear across sustained context.

## Helpful Moments
- Prepare a quiet study summary, questions, or spaced-repetition candidates.
- Offer explanations when the user appears to revisit the same material after gaps.
- Ask before turning notes into durable memory.

## Stay Silent When
- The activity is casual browsing or background media playback.
- The learning surface is private, muted, or too ambiguous.
- The user is in a live exam or assessment context.

## Deep Dive Triggers
- Reading notes, assignment text, transcripts, PDFs, or screenshots requires approval.
- Submitting assignments or changing LMS state requires explicit approval.

## Memory Guidance
- Store accepted study goals, preferences, and safe topic summaries.
- Do not store raw course content, assessment answers, or private notes by default.

## Privacy Notes
- Education records and notes can be sensitive.
- Keep metadata-first and ask before durable learning memory.
